\begin{abstract}
\onehalfspacing
We present the Casimir-Gravity Crossover (CGC) framework---a phenomenological \textit{ansatz} motivated by vacuum energy physics that modifies gravitational interactions on cosmological scales. The framework introduces an environment-dependent gravitational enhancement reaching 14.9\% in low-density regions while preserving standard gravity in screened high-density environments. Through Markov Chain Monte Carlo analysis of Planck 2018 CMB, BOSS DR12 BAO, Pantheon+ supernovae, and RSD growth data, we constrain the coupling parameter to $\mu = 0.149 \pm 0.025$ (6$\sigma$ from null). Within this framework, the Hubble tension reduces from 4.8$\sigma$ to 1.9$\sigma$ (61\% reduction) and the $S_8$ tension from 3.1$\sigma$ to 0.6$\sigma$ (82\% reduction).

\textbf{Two-front falsification strategy:} (1) \textit{CGC Tabletop Validation Experiment (immediate):} A laboratory experiment exploiting the Casimir effect---the quantum vacuum force first predicted by Hendrik Casimir in 1948---with gold plates predicts a vacuum-gravity crossover at separation $d_c \approx 95\,\mu$m, testable with current AFM technology. This experiment leverages Casimir's established physics as the probe, while the \textit{crossover} is the novel CGC prediction. (2) \textit{Cosmological test (2029):} CGC predicts 10\% scale-dependent growth enhancement, testable by DESI Year 5 at $>5\sigma$ precision. If the fundamental CGC mechanism is correct, the laboratory experiment must succeed. If it fails, CGC remains a valid phenomenological description but lacks microscopic foundation. \textbf{Either cosmological failure constitutes definitive exclusion.}
\end{abstract}

The Casimir effect demonstrates that quantum vacuum fluctuations produce measurable forces. Between parallel conducting plates separated by distance $d$:

\begin{equation}
P_{\text{Casimir}} = \frac{\pi^2 \hbar c}{240 d^4}
\label{eq:casimir}
\end{equation}

where $P$ is the pressure (force per unit area). This attractive force arises from the difference in vacuum mode structure inside versus outside the plates.

\subsection{Why a Power Law? Effective Field Theory Justification}

The scale-dependent function $f(k) = (k/k_{\text{pivot}})^{n_g}$ requires justification beyond curve-fitting.

\begin{mechanism}[EFT Argument for Power-Law Scaling]
In effective field theory, modifications to gravity at low energies generically introduce corrections expandable in powers of the momentum scale $k$:

\begin{equation}
\frac{G_{\text{eff}}(k)}{G_N} = 1 + c_1 \left(\frac{k}{k_*}\right)^{n_1} + c_2 \left(\frac{k}{k_*}\right)^{n_2} + \cdots
\end{equation}

where $k_*$ is a characteristic cutoff scale. The \textbf{leading-order correction} dominates at cosmological scales where $k \ll k_*$. Our ansatz captures this leading term with:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item $c_1 \to \mu$ (amplitude)
    \item $n_1 \to n_g$ (exponent, constrained to $n_g = 0.138 \pm 0.014$)
\end{itemize}

The small value $n_g \approx 0.14$ indicates near-logarithmic running, consistent with one-loop quantum corrections in scalar-tensor theories.
\end{mechanism}

This does \textit{not} derive $\mu$ from first principles, but it provides theoretical motivation for the functional form.

\subsection{The Coupling Parameter $\mu$: What It Represents}

The best-fit value $\mu = 0.149 \pm 0.025$ represents a 14.9\% maximum enhancement of gravity. We interpret this as:

\begin{equation}
\mu \sim \frac{\rho_{\text{vacuum,eff}}}{\rho_{\text{grav}}} \sim \frac{\hbar c / L_{\text{void}}^4}{G_N M_{\text{void}} / L_{\text{void}}^2}
\end{equation}

For void size $L_{\text{void}} \sim 50$ Mpc and typical void mass deficit, order-of-magnitude estimates yield $\mu \sim O(0.1)$---consistent with (but not derived from) the fitted value.

\textbf{Status:} We present $\mu$ as an \textit{empirically constrained effective parameter}. Its consistency with vacuum energy estimates is encouraging but not definitive.

\begin{mechanism}[Hendrik Casimir and Established Physics]
The Casimir effect was predicted by Dutch physicist \textbf{Hendrik Casimir} in 1948 and experimentally confirmed by Lamoreaux in 1997. It describes the attractive force between uncharged conducting plates arising from quantum vacuum fluctuations---\textit{this is established, Nobel-prize-winning physics}.

\textbf{What is established (Casimir):} The vacuum force $F_{\text{Cas}} \propto \hbar c / d^4$ between parallel plates.

\textbf{What is new (CGC):} The prediction that at separation $d_c$, where Casimir and gravitational forces become comparable, a \textit{deviation} from standard scaling should occur if vacuum fluctuations couple to gravity as hypothesized in CGC.

The experiment uses Casimir's established physics as a \textit{precision probe}; the novel prediction is the crossover behavior.
\end{mechanism}

\begin{equation}

In Fourier space, each term contributes $\propto k^{2n}$, with the leading correction determining $n_g$.

\subsection{Casimir-Gravity Crossover Derivation}

The crossover distance $d_c$ is derived from equating Casimir and gravitational pressures between parallel plates:

\textbf{Casimir pressure:}
\begin{equation}
P_{\text{Cas}} = \frac{\pi^2 \hbar c}{240 d^4}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Gravitational pressure (infinite parallel plates):}
\begin{equation}
P_{\text{grav}} = 2\pi G \sigma^2
\end{equation}

where $\sigma = \rho t$ is surface mass density.

\textbf{Crossover condition:}
\begin{equation}
\frac{\pi^2 \hbar c}{240 d_c^4} = 2\pi G \sigma^2
\end{equation}

Solving:
\begin{equation}
d_c^4 = \frac{\pi \hbar c}{480 G \sigma^2} \quad \Rightarrow \quad d_c = \left(\frac{\pi \hbar c}{480 G \sigma^2}\right)^{1/4}
\end{equation}

Numerically: $d_c = 1.327 \times 10^{-5} \times \sigma^{-1/2}$ meters.
